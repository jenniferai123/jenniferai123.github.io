About Me

I am in my second year at Cornell University studying computing and information Sciences in the college of engineering. I have always been fascinated with technology and aesthetics, so throughout I have steadily garnered an interest in coding and designing.

Originally from a suburb north of Dallas, TX, I loved being creative through art and crochet while shifting to thinking logically while completing computer science and data science coursework. At Cornell, I am a part of the Outreach team at Women in Computing at Cornell. Each week, I dedicate a few hours of my time to teaching middle school and high school students how to code in Python.

In my free time I love to make coffee, watch A24 movies, and listen to soft indie. Recently, my favorite artists have been Japanese Breakfast, Men I Trust, and Taylor Swift. I also run a small foodie account on Instagram!
